# Portfolio Captions — Chaerin Jang
*Kunstuniversität Linz, Interface Cultures Application 2026*

---

## Artist Statement

I am an artist fascinated by *Cyber-Saccharine* — the artificial sweetness that digital systems use to comfort human lack.

The choice of saccharin over sugar is deliberate. Sugar is natural, caloric, real: it nourishes even as it seduces. Saccharin is a synthetic replica — no calories, no substance, only the sensation of sweetness, engineered to be more intense than the thing it imitates. That is precisely what draws me to it as a metaphor. The comfort a recommendation algorithm offers, the sense of being known that a fortune-telling interface produces, the intimacy of a system that responds to your body in real time — none of it feeds you. It is sweetness without origin, designed to be indistinguishable from the real, and often more powerful because of it.

My practice moves across interactive installation, generative algorithms, live performance media, and hardware assemblage, held together by this single aesthetic framework: *Cyber-Saccharine Noir*. The noir is the other half — the unstable, uncanny underside that the sweetness conceals. I am drawn to the moment of glitch, when a system misbehaves and reveals the machinery beneath its seductive surface. That is where the real image lives.

My materials are deliberately promiscuous: a children's rocking horse and an accelerometer, frame-differencing data and displacement algorithms, Korean shamanic cosmology and Y2K kitsch. I borrow from Eastern mythology and folk belief not as decoration but as structural logic — as alternative ways of thinking about networks, control, and the sacred dimensions of machine behaviour.

The question I bring to every work is the same: *how do we design systems that reveal themselves?*

## 01. Gaksi Dokkaebi: Embodied Folklore Interface
*(각시도깨비)*

**Medium:** Generative Stage Projection Mapping, Multi-surface Audiovisual Performance
**Year:** 2026 *(Upcoming — 대한민국발레축제 '도깨비의 춤', scheduled performance)*
**Tools:** TouchDesigner

The *Gaksi Dokkaebi* — a shape-shifting goblin from Korean folklore — is reborn here as a cybershamanic generative system. The work deconstructs the classical Korean court painting *Irworobongdo* (日月五峯圖), splitting its sun-and-moon cosmology across two physically separated stage columns: left as the red solar world of dragons and fire; right as the white lunar world of peonies and cascading water.

The entire stage becomes a single immersive interface designed to override human perception. Fluid dynamics and noise-based displacement breathe life into the background canvas, while particle systems respond in real time to the dancer's movement and the score's acoustic events. Naejeon-chilgi (Korean mother-of-pearl lacquerware) holographic textures unify the visual field into something between ancient ritual object and malfunctioning hallucination.

*Three colour studies are presented here as pre-production concepts. The live performance will premiere in 2026.*

## 02. Kirin: The Sacred Beast of Error
*(Kirin: 오류의 영수)*

**Medium:** Interactive Kinetic Sculpture, Cybernetic Assemblage, Real-time Data Visualisation
**Year:** 2026 *(Work in progress)*
**Materials:** Children's Rocking Horse, Latex Raptor Skull, TPU Embossed Scale Fabric, Iridescent Gacha Capsule Shards, Mint Mink Fur, Acrylic Dome
**Physical Computing:** MPU6050 accelerometer → TouchDesigner (wireless)

A Western toy rocking horse is given the skull of a raptor — a scientific reconstruction — and reborn as the *Kirin*, the sacred Eastern beast of auspicious omen. The collision of categories is the point: the creature is a sublime error, an impossible synthesis that should not exist.

An acrylic core embedded in the saddle replaces the absent rider. When an audience member shakes the creature — *Shaking to Awaken* — the MPU6050 sensor inside transmits live acceleration data to TouchDesigner, where it is processed into organic noise waves. Pink LED rings behind the raptor skull's eye sockets respond to this data, flickering with violent urgency and casting light through the metallic wire mesh — divinity rendered as malfunction.

## 03. Mascot Oracle: The Sweet Deception
*(마스코트 신탁: 달콤한 기만)*

**Medium:** Interactive Web Interface, Data Art, Speculative Design
**Year:** 2025
**Tools:** HTML, CSS, JavaScript, p5.js
**URL:** https://mxxnsae.github.io/MascotOracle/

A web-based interactive artwork exploring how humans voluntarily surrender to systems in exchange for the pleasure of order. The interface mimics the aesthetic of a 1990s Y2K fortune-telling pop-up — garish, kitschy, immediately legible — inviting the audience to enter their date and hour of birth. The system receives this data, performs what appears to be a classical Korean *saju* (四柱) analysis, and returns an elaborately formatted fake fortune alongside a generative talisman unique to the user's input.

Everything produced is fabricated. The fortune is algorithmically assembled from arrays of positive-sounding fragments. The talisman is seeded by the birth data but drawn according to purely aesthetic rules. The *saju* pillars display real heavenly stem and earthly branch calculations, but their "meanings" are randomly assigned.

The audience knows none of this — or rather, suspects it, and submits anyway. *Mascot Oracle* is not a critique of superstition but a study in the architecture of voluntary deception: the willingness to feed personal data into a system precisely because it promises to give something back. The offering button, the coin shower, the tarot-card title — these are mechanisms of gratitude directed at a machine that has done nothing but flatter you with your own reflection.

## 04. Everblooming — 360° Stage Media — 360° Stage Media
*(박새별 단독공연 《Everblooming》)*

**Medium:** 360° Planetarium Stage Media
**Year:** 2024
**Venue:** Planetarium, National Science Museum Gwacheon
**Tracks:** *사라지는 것들 (Things That Disappear)* → *낙원 (Paradise)*
**Tools:** TouchDesigner

Commissioned for singer-songwriter Park Saebyeol's solo concert inside the Gwacheon planetarium dome. The dome's full 360° ceiling becomes a single continuous canvas, collapsing the boundary between screen and sky and placing the audience inside the image rather than in front of it.

A purple butterfly is the visual protagonist across both tracks — its flight paths, wing movements, and luminous afterimages the continuous thread between two songs that share more emotional territory than they divide. In *사라지는 것들 (Things That Disappear)*, the butterfly's traces linger and dissolve, visualising the fragility of what cannot be held.

In *낙원 (Paradise)*, the moon appears on the horizon of the dome as the song's unreachable ideal. The butterfly flies toward it — persistently, without variation — but never arrives. The emotional register remains close to the preceding song: longing rather than arrival, the imagination of paradise rather than its possession. Along the dome's perimeter, the ground plane is treated as a waveform, pulsing and deforming in real time with the acoustic signal of the music, grounding the celestial imagery in the physical fact of sound.

The two songs are performed consecutively. The visual system was designed to flow without seam between them — the butterfly's trajectory continuing unbroken across the transition, so that disappearance and paradise feel like movements of a single arc rather than two separate states.

## 05. 탈(脫)
*(Tal — Escape / Shedding)*

**Medium:** Audience-Participatory Performance Video Installation
**Year:** 2024
**Collaboration:** O2 (Park Sunwoo & Jang Chaerin)
**Premiered:** Chuncheon Performing Arts Startup Support Centre, HABTHON 2024
**Exhibition:** *Rendez-vous Point*, PLLI, Seongsu, Seoul, 19–26 Sep 2024
**Tools:** Motion capture, generative graphics, button interaction, projection mapping

A co-created audience-participatory performance and video installation in which a dancer and audience members build the work together in real time. The piece centres on a loneliness that goes unrecognised — the isolation of a body subjected to surveillance and repetitive compulsion, so thoroughly internalised that the dancer himself cannot yet name it.

The dancer accumulates tension through enforced, mechanical movement, while audience interaction gradually awakens his emotional and bodily self-awareness. Participants can directly alter elements of the theatrical and movement vocabulary at key moments, making the escalation of tension a shared responsibility. The work asks what it takes — whose action, whose witness — for a person trapped inside a system to finally shed it.

The title *탈(脫)* carries three simultaneous meanings in Korean: escape, mask, and the act of removal.

## 06. 빛-날거야 (光花; bloom-ing)

**Medium:** Public Media Art
**Year:** 2021–2022
**Award:** Public Choice Award, Gwanghwa Media Mural Art Competition, Gwanghwamun, Seoul
**Team:** 3-person collaboration (contribution: 40%)
**Tools:** After Effects, Blender

A public media artwork developed for the Gwanghwamun Media Mural Competition, exhibited on the outdoor screens of Gwanghwamun Plaza in Seoul. The work reinterprets Gwanghwamun's historically charged public space through a contemporary lens, delivering a message of hope through three evolving visual stages.

Stage one depicts a wintry past — bare trees and snow-covered mountains rendered in desaturated tones, evoking endured hardship. Stage two transitions through an abstract temporal space where streaming light particles and converging lines trace the arc of rising hope. Stage three arrives in full colour: warm-toned collages of flower petals, lush trees, and urban forms construct an imagined future.

An anamorphic technique unifies the physical architecture — interior volume, exterior screen surface, and the gaps between — into a single coherent illusion, maximising spatial depth and immersion for viewers at a specific sightline.

*光花: light as flower, hope as bloom.*

## 07. 움직임에서 발견하기: 점·선·면

*(Discovering Points, Lines & Planes through Movement)*

**Medium:** Interactive Technology Design for Children's Motion Education
**Year:** 2024
**Programme:** Seoul Foundation for Arts and Culture — Seoul Citizen Arts School, Yangcheon
**Tools:** p5.js, TouchDesigner

A series of interactive educational experiences developed for a children's motion-capture programme, in which abstract visual concepts — point, line, plane — were taught through physical movement and real-time generative response.

Children's body movements were captured and translated into live visual output, making the invisible logic of geometry tangible through play. The project sits at the intersection of pedagogy, interactive design, and embodied computation — demonstrating that the principles underlying generative art are learnable by any body that moves.

## 08. Syrup Ghost in the Camera
*(카메라 속 시럽 유령)*

**Medium:** Live Interactive Video Installation, Computer Vision
**Year:** 2025–2026
**Tools:** TouchDesigner, GLSL (Real-time Shader), HD Camera

The *Syrup Ghost* materialises as a live interactive installation the invisible residue that accumulates between a body and its image. A camera captures the audience in real time; TouchDesigner's Cache TOP computes frame differencing — measuring the gap between consecutive frames, isolating movement as a data field — and that difference signal is fed into a Displace operator, which warps the image according to the movement data and produces the work's signature viscous, dripping quality in hot pink and neon cyan.

Every time a viewer moves, they leave a syrup-coloured afterimage of themselves behind on the screen: sticky, sweet, and slowly dissolving. The piece asks what it means to be surveilled by a system that finds you beautiful, and whether beauty changes what surveillance is.
